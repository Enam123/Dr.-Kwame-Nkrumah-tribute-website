Osagyefo Dr. Kwame Nkrumah

A brief time line of Dr. Nkrumah:

  • 1909- Born on the 21st of September in Nkroful, Gold Coast (in Ghana)
  • 1925- He became a student-teacher at the Government Training College (Achimota School), an elimentary missionary school, after his mother sent him there since he was her only child.
  • 1931- After obtaining his teacher's certificate at Prince of Wales' College a year before, he was given a teaching post at the Roman Catholic primary school in Elmina.
  • 1935- Influenced by Nnamdi Azikiwe, he arrived in the USA to enroll at the Lincoln University.
  • 1939- Completed Bachelor of Arts degree in economics and sociology.
  • 1945- Arrived in the UK to further his education.
  • 1947- Arrived in the Gold Coast to accept the office of general secretary of the United Gold Coast Conversion (UGCC).
  • 1948- Along Danquah, they led a massive historic demonstration known as the 1948 Accra riots.
  • 1949- Formed his on party after he was pressured considerably by his supporters.
  • 1951- Rose to the office of the Leader of Government Business after being elected in the first general election to be held under universal franchise in colonial Africa.
  • 1952- Became Prime Minister after the then Governor, Arden-Clarke, withdrew from the cabinet.
  • 1957- Help Gold Coast to gain independence.
  • 1960- Gold Coast became a Republic under the name he suggested, Ghana and he became the first president.
  • 1966- Was overthrown on a state visit to North Vietnam and China, in a violent coup d'etat led by the national military and police forces, with backing from the civil service
  • 1972- He died at the age of 62 while in Romania after he lived in exile in Conakry, Guinea, as the guest of President Ahmed Sékou Touré, who made him honorary co-president of the country

"Divided we are weak. United, Africa could become one of the greatest forces for good in the world."
